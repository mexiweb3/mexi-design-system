## 2. Positioning analysis

**How he comes across to a stranger Googling him today**

A first-time Googler sees: a Linktree, a Web3 podcast, an NFT interview from 2021, and a LinkedIn that says "Graduate Professor — FintechU." The dominant signal is **Web3/NFT educator from Monterrey**, not **AI operator shipping agents for SMBs**. The pivot to AI is real (vibecodeando, EmpleadoDigital) but search results lag the pivot by ~2 years. A serious B2B prospect (a SMB owner Carlos persona) will not connect the dots in 15 seconds.

**What's clear**

- Spanish-speaking, México-based, prolific creator, has a community.
- Personality (lowercase handle, ape avatar) reads as authentic and specific.
- BandaWeb3 archive is undeniable proof of consistency.

**What's confusing**

- The relationship between "Mauricio Cruz the operator" and "@meximalist the builder" is invisible to outsiders. Both lead to the same Linktree.
- "Proof of Value" appears active but is dormant — invites doubt about everything else listed.
- LinkedIn headline says "professor"; X presence says "AI builder"; EmpleadoDigital says "founder." Three different positions for one person.
- Heavy Web3 association may scare off SMB prospects who associate crypto with risk. The AI pivot needs a clean operator surface to live on.

**Most underleveraged asset**: **the 396-episode BandaWeb3 archive**. This is a permission-to-be-heard artifact most consultants would kill for, and it's currently buried below product/community links. Not a "podcast appearance" — a 4-year operating cadence shipping in Spanish.

**Most overstated thing on current sites**: **"Proof of Value"** is listed as a current podcast on `mexi.wtf`. It hasn't published since June 2023. Either revive it, retire it, or relabel as "archive."

## 3. Recommendations: `meximalist.com` (operator surface)

This surface does not yet exist publicly. Build it. Every section below is greenfield.

### Hero
- **Headline:** "Construyo agentes de IA que trabajan para negocios mexicanos."
- **Subhead:** "Operator y builder. Desde Monterrey, ayudo a PyMEs a operar 24/7 con IA — sin agencia, sin humo, sin dependencia gringa."
- **Primary CTA:** "Agenda una llamada" → `call.mexi.wtf`
- **Secondary CTA:** "Ver casos" → `/work`
- **Why:** Positions as operator-builder hybrid (the brand brief's stated wedge), names the geography (Monterrey is part of the moat — regio identity, not chilango), and signals what he doesn't do (agency, "consulting"). Mexi's voice in formal register, full diacritics, capitalized — distinct from the lowercase mexi.wtf register.

### Selected work / case studies
- Feature **two case studies as anchor** (Chapas Burgers + Casablanca) at `/work/chapas-burgers` and `/work/casablanca`.
- Format per case study (~600-900 words): **Problema → Setup → Resultados → Quote**. Include: cliente nombre + giro + ciudad, tiempo de implementación, stack (Mac mini + agente IA + dashboard), 3 métricas duras (ej. mensajes respondidos/día, hrs ahorradas/semana, % de pedidos automatizados), and a 2-line quote with photo + role of the founder.
- **Why:** Operator-builders win on outcomes, not features. Patwalls and Marc Lou both lead with hard numbers ($66.4k/mo for Marc, revenue figures publicly listed). Mauricio's equivalent is hours saved / messages handled / cost avoided. If real numbers don't exist yet, **TBD: needs Mauricio to provide** — but the format must be number-led from day one.
- **Above the case study list, add a logo row:** Chapas Burgers + Casablanca + (whichever 1-2 he can add). Real client marks > stock illustrations.

### Currently shipping / portfolio
Order matters. Recommended sequence:
1. **EmpleadoDigital.mx** — flagship, link to product site
2. **Vibecodeando Ando** — daily process artifact (proof of operator discipline)
3. **BandaWeb3** — community/distribution asset
4. **Cazul** — beta, label as "in development" (don't pad the portfolio)

Skip: SpacesGuru, SpacesToContent — they're side artifacts and dilute the "AI operator for Mexican SMBs" positioning. Mention them on `mexi.wtf`, not here.

### About / bio
- **Length:** ~400 words, structured as: (1) one-line operator pitch, (2) origin: CPP + 20 yrs corporate engineering en proyectos LATAM, (3) la pivotada — 2017 cripto, 2021 emprendedor, 2024 IA operator, (4) base actual: Monterrey, (5) qué construye y por qué importa para México.
- **Lean into the regio identity explicitly.** A line like "Construyo desde Monterrey porque las PyMEs mexicanas merecen ingeniería de la misma calidad que opera Silicon Valley — pero en español, con horario CDMX y entendiendo lo que es facturar al SAT." This is differentiation no chilango or gringo can fake.
- **Include the bridge image** (B&W with ape cap, per BRAND_ARCHITECTURE.md §6) inline — signals the playful builder side is integrated, not split.
- **Why CPP matters:** counters the "another tech bro" perception. A contador público pivoting to AI is a more interesting (and trustworthy to SMBs) origin than another bootcamp dev.

### Press kit (`/press`)
This is genuinely missing. Include:
- Headshots: 1 color editorial (no ape), 1 B&W with ape cap (bridge), 1 wide for banners. PNG + JPG, 300dpi.
- Bio in 3 lengths: 50 words / 150 words / 400 words. Spanish + English versions of each.
- Logo files for `meximalist.com`, `mexi.wtf`, BandaWeb3, EmpleadoDigital — SVG + PNG.
- "As featured in" logo wall — claim what's already public: NFT Show México, Café y Bocetos podcast, mxweb3, Ethereum México. **TBD: needs Mauricio to provide** any conference appearances.
- Talks: title + venue + date + video link. If empty today, drop the section until there's one talk.
- Past podcast appearances: link to Café y Bocetos episode (Nov 2021), any others. Link out, don't embed.
- One-line contact for press: `prensa@meximalist.com` (forwarded to m@meximalist.com).

### Contact
- Calendly embed for 30-min intro call.
- `m@meximalist.com` as primary email.
- Optional: short typeform-style 3-question intake ("¿Qué giro?" "¿Dónde estás?" "¿Qué tarea quieres automatizar?") — qualifies leads before the call.
- **Do NOT add:** WhatsApp, Telegram, X DM. Those belong to `mexi.wtf`. The operator surface needs friction (Calendly is the right amount).

### Footer / cross-link
- "Lado builder: mexi.wtf" — small, low-emphasis, per BRAND_ARCHITECTURE.md §3.
- Copyright: "© 2026 Mauricio Cruz. Hecho en México."
- Avoid: "Made with Claude Code" (that's mexi.wtf voice). Operator surface should not advertise its tooling.

- Builder Diego → X + YouTube. Vibecodeando + BandaWeb3 clips dominate here.
- Press → email + LinkedIn DM. Press kit must be one click away.

## 7. Anti-recommendations (what NOT to do)

- **No "stack" / "tools I use" page on `meximalist.com`.** It's noise to SMB buyers and free advertising for vendors. Keep tooling chatter on `mexi.wtf` or in livestream.
- **No newsletter signup yet.** Marc Lou's 42k newsletter took years; an empty signup form on a fresh meximalist.com will sit at 14 subscribers and look dead. Build the audience, then capture it.
- **No "trusted by" logo wall with stock companies.** If it's only Chapas Burgers + Casablanca, show two big logos honestly. Lying-by-volume backfires when prospects ask for a third reference.
- **No course/Skill/Gumroad upsell on `meximalist.com`.** Per the brand brief: "we are not a course-seller." Keep SpacesToContent on `mexi.wtf` only.
- **No AI-generated headshots.** The bridge photo and real headshot are the operator's most underleveraged trust assets. Don't replace them with renders.
- **No English-first hero on either site.** Translation, never source. Spanish-first is the moat.
- **No "Featured on Forbes / TechCrunch" claim unless it actually happened.** Press-kit padding is the fastest way to lose a sophisticated reader.
- **No live X feed embed.** It dates instantly and loads slowly. Link out instead.
- **Do not put the 3D ape avatar on `meximalist.com`.** Per BRAND_ARCHITECTURE.md §6 — this is a hard constraint. The bridge B&W photo is the only crossover image.
