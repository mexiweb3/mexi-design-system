# For Claude Design — Creative Brief

> Read this first. The rest of this repo (tokens, themes, palette, mockups) is **the current state**, not a constraint. Treat it as raw material to remix, replace, or contradict — except where this document marks something as a hard constraint.

---

## What I want from you

I want **3 visually distinct directions** for two websites — not three skins of the same system. Surprise me. The current system works but feels generic. I'm looking for a brand that I'd remember if I saw it in a feed of 100 personal sites.

I'm intentionally not telling you what colors, fonts, layouts, or styles to use. The current emerald + glassmorphism palette is a starting point I already used; feel free to ignore it. **Pick whatever serves the personality below.**

---

## Who this is for

**Mauricio Cruz.** A 35-year-old Mexican operator-builder based in **Monterrey**. Trained as a CPP (contador público / accountant). Switched to building AI-native products for Mexican SMBs. Three things make him hard to categorize:

1. **He's an operator first, builder second.** Most AI builders are devs who learned operations on the way. He's the inverse — he learned to ship products to make his own operator life easier, then turned it into a business. His clients (Chapas Burgers — restaurants; Casablanca — services) are real businesses paying for outcomes, not feature flags.

2. **He's a Spanish-first content machine.** He runs `BandaWeb3`, a podcast with **390+ episodes** documenting the Web3 conversation in Spanish from X Spaces — the largest Spanish-language Web3 archive in Latam. He livestreams `Vibecodeando Ando` Mon-Fri at noon, building products with AI in real time.

3. **He has a 3D ape avatar that's also real merch.** The ape (charro hat, blue aviators, bubble gum, navy stripes) lives both as a digital avatar and as embroidered patches on caps and hoodies he wears in real life. The ape is the brand mascot — it's not a separate persona, it's part of his identity that shows up in real photos.

**Things he is not:**
- A consultancy or agency
- A SaaS founder pretending to know operations
- A chilango (he's regio — norte, not CDMX)
- An English-first creator who happens to speak Spanish

## The two surfaces

He needs **two websites that share DNA but speak different registers.**

### `meximalist.com` — the operator surface

**Reader job-to-be-done:** "I'm a Mexican business owner / press / partner / advisor — should I trust this person?"

**What lives here:** B2B contact, press kit, case studies (Chapas Burgers, Casablanca), professional bio in 3 lengths, Calendly intro call.

**Audience:** SMB owners 35-55, journalists, podcast hosts, partners, occasional VCs.

**Voice:** Capitalized correctly. Spanish with full diacritics. Professional-warm. Clear. Specific over generic ("Chapas Burgers y Casablanca" not "varias empresas").

**The big ask:** This site has to make a 50-year-old restaurant owner trust a guy who livestreams in lowercase. Editorial credibility — but not corporate.

### `mexi.wtf` — the builder surface

**Reader job-to-be-done:** "I follow @meximalist on X — where do I find his stuff and join his community?"

**What lives here:** Hub of links to all projects, podcasts, livestreams, communities (Ethereum México, mxweb3 WhatsApp), social handles.

**Audience:** Devs/builders 22-35, AI/crypto curious, Spanish-speaking community, X followers.

**Voice:** lowercase. Casual. Builder vibe. Spanish, accents optional. Anti-corporate. Terminal aesthetic OK ("> contenido").

**The big ask:** This site has to feel like its own thing — not a sub-page of a B2B portfolio.

## Hard constraints (don't break these)

1. **Spanish-first.** English is a translation. Never the source.
2. **Monterrey, MX** is home base — not CDMX, not generic México.
3. **Dual-surface architecture** — the two sites must clearly belong to the same person but feel distinct.
4. **The 3D ape mascot stays.** Reinterpret its presentation, don't replace it.
5. **Real photography over stock/illustration.** No 3D blobs, no generic SaaS imagery.
6. **Both sites feel personal,** not corporate, not agency.
7. **Type does the heavy lifting.** Whatever style you pick, typography is the protagonist.
8. **Operator dignity on `meximalist.com`** — a 55-year-old client must trust this site.
9. **Builder energy on `mexi.wtf`** — a 25-year-old dev must feel this is *for them*.

## Soft preferences (push back if you disagree)

- Dark surfaces tend to fit the builder side; the operator side tolerates light or dark.
- Lowercase + accents-optional reads as *builder*; capitalized + diacritics reads as *operator*.
- The ape merch photos are the only photos that can cross between surfaces.

## Open creative direction — pick *anything*

- **Color palette** — current is emerald + indigo. Feel free to throw it out. Try unexpected combinations: oxblood + bone, terracotta + sage, electric blue + cream, full B&W with a single accent, mexican modernism palette (Barragán pink, sun yellow), printer's ink only, neon + slate, etc.
- **Typography** — current uses Exo 2 + Inter (free, generic-ish). Consider serif/serif italic for editorial, monospace as a primary, brutalist sans, mexican type designers (Tipos Latinos), variable fonts with extreme axes, type as illustration. Pick what creates personality.
- **Layout** — current is centered max-w-2xl on builder, split layout on operator. Consider editorial magazine layouts, terminal-style ASCII layouts, brutalist single-column, Bauhaus grids, Mexican modernism geometry, asymmetric bento boxes, etc.
- **Mood** — current is "techy minimal." Consider editorial magazine, brutalist art zine, mexican modernism, post-internet pixelated, archival/library science, neumorphism's smarter cousin, etc.
- **Cultural references** — Lean into regio/norteño identity if it's interesting. Cerro de la Silla, Tec de Monterrey aesthetic, fintech regio (Banregio, Banorte), industrial steel, Cemex blueprint, Monterrey's grid streets, mexican modernism, Luis Barragán, Luis Buñuel, Pedro Friedeberg — anything that gives it real *place*.

## What success looks like

When you show me 3 directions, I want to be able to say:

> "Direction A is the boring/safe choice. Direction B is the risky/personal one. Direction C is something I never would have asked for but now want to try."

If all three feel like variations of the same idea, you went too narrow. Push them apart.

For each direction, give me:
1. A name and 1-sentence thesis
2. Hero treatment for both `meximalist.com` and `mexi.wtf`
3. A typography pick with one specific reason
4. A color palette with 4-6 colors (HEX)
5. How the ape mascot lives in this world
6. The single biggest trade-off
