# Voice — mexi.wtf (@meximalist)

> Builder. Casual. Lowercase. Direct. Anti-corporate.

---

## Core principles

1. **lowercase as default** — except brand names (BandaWeb3, EmpleadoDigital), proper nouns when ambiguity matters, badges (LIVE, BETA), and abbreviations (IA, CDMX, CEO).
2. **Acentos opcionales** — both "construyendo con ia desde mexico" and "construyendo con IA desde México" are valid. Pick one and stay consistent within a single page.
3. **Direct over polite.** Skip "estamos emocionados de anunciar". Just say what it is.
4. **Builder energy** — "vamos a romperla", "ya esta listo", "lo subi anoche".
5. **Spanish first.** English is a translation, never the source.
6. **No emojis as decoration.** The `>` prompt symbol is the only recurring visual flourish in copy.

---

## Vocabulary

### Use
- builder, building, construir, ship, shipping, en vivo, livestream
- IA (not "inteligencia artificial"), Web3, vibe, vibecoding
- comunidad, banda, raza, gente
- "Construyendo con IA desde México" (signature)

### Avoid
- "Innovador", "soluciones", "transformación digital", "ecosistema"
- "Nuestro equipo de expertos" — there's no "team", solo Mauricio
- "Revolucionario", "disruptivo", "next-gen"
- Emojis salpicados en titulos
- Frases de LinkedIn ("emocionado de compartir...")

## Length rules

- **Hero headline:** 4-7 words max
- **Project description:** 1-2 sentences max
- **Section header:** 1 word
- **CTA button:** 2-4 words
- **Card subtitle:** 8-15 words

If you need more than this, the structure is wrong, not the copy.

## Bilingual rules

When translating to English on `/en/`:
- Keep lowercase
- Keep brevity
- "construyendo con ia desde mexico" → "building with ai from mexico"
- Brand names stay (BandaWeb3, EmpleadoDigital, never translated)
- "vibecodeando ando" stays in Spanish (it's a brand)
