> Operator. Professional-warm. Capitalized. Spanish neutro con acentos. Clear.

---

## Core principles

1. **Capitalize correctly.** First letter of sentence, proper nouns, brand names, acronyms.
2. **Full diacritics.** "México" not "Mexico". "está" not "esta". "construí" not "construi".
3. **Concise but complete.** Sentences end with periods. No fragments unless intentional.
4. **Professional-warm.** Not corporate, not casual. Think "first email to a respected colleague".
5. **Specific over generic.** Say "Chapas Burgers y Casablanca", not "varias empresas mexicanas".
6. **Spanish primary, English available.** English is a translation, but written natively (not direct calques).

## Vocabulary

### Use
- Operator, builder, construyo, opero, construyendo
- Inteligencia artificial / IA (both acceptable; IA on second mention)
- Empresas mexicanas, PyMEs, negocios, founders
- Advisory, asesoría, consultoría selectiva
- Caso de estudio, resultado, métrica
- "Construyo agentes de IA para empresas mexicanas." (signature)

### Avoid (same as builder voice, plus)
- Lowercase as a stylistic choice
- Slang regional pesado ("la neta", "no manches")
- "Solo Mauricio Cruz" — it's understood, no need to underline
- Apologetic phrasing ("disculpa la molestia", "si no es mucho pedir")
- Emojis (zero)
- Excessive enthusiasm ("¡increíble!", "¡fantástico!")

## Length rules

- **Hero headline:** Name only ("Mauricio Cruz") OR 3-5 words ("Operator y builder")
- **Hero subtitle:** 1 sentence, ≤ 20 words
- **Project description:** 2-3 sentences max, lead with outcome (clientes, métricas)
- **CTA button:** 2-4 words, verb-led
- **Bio paragraph:** 3-4 sentences max in primary, longer versions in `/about`
